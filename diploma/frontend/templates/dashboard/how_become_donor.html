How to Become a Donor?

  1. Make sure you don't have any contraindications to donating blood.
  2. Choose the nearest blood center, check their needs by phone, and schedule a donation. If you're not yet registered on DonorSearch, sign up so we can remind you of your appointment and send recommendations.
  3. On the scheduled day, go to the blood center — don't forget your passport! (Some regions may require local registration.)
  4. After your donation, you'll receive a certificate — take a photo and upload it to your personal account. Once verified, your donation will appear in your profile and you'll receive bonuses from friends and project partners.

How to Prepare for Blood Donation?

  • Avoid fatty, fried, spicy, and smoked foods on the day before and the day of donation. Avoid dairy, eggs, oils, nuts, dates, bananas, and avocados.
  • For 48 hours before donation, do not consume alcohol. For 72 hours, avoid medications like aspirin and analgesics.
  • Eat a light breakfast in the morning. Do not donate blood on an empty stomach!

What to Do After Donating Blood?

  • If you feel dizzy or weak, inform the staff. Lie down and raise your legs above head level or sit and put your head between your knees.
  • Avoid smoking for at least 2 hours before and after donation.
  • Keep the bandage on for 3–4 hours and avoid getting it wet.
  • Avoid heavy physical activity for 24 hours.
  • Do not drink alcohol for 24 hours.
  • Eat well and drink plenty of fluids for 2 days.
  • You may receive vaccinations 10 days after donation.
  • You cannot donate blood immediately after scuba diving.
  • Do not ride a motorcycle for 2 hours after donation.
